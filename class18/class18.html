Q. Add some major milestones including the first wP vaccine roll-out (1946), the switch to the newer aP vaccine (of 1996), the COVID years (2020)?

ggplot(cdc) +
  aes(year, cases) +
  geom_point() +
  geom_line() +
  geom_vline(xintercept = 1946, col="blue", lty=2) + 
  geom_vline(xintercept = 1996, col="red", lty=2) + 
  geom_vline(xintercept = 2020, col="gray", lty=2)

There were high case numbers in the pre-1940s, then the introduction of the first wP vaccine in 1946 lowered the case numbers and then the case numbers got even lower with the introduction of the second aP vaccine in 1996. However in the mid-2000s, the case numbers increased due to circulating myths regarding vaccines and more people lacking the booster update shots but the case numbers lowered in 2020 due to quarantine and lockdown mandates which prevented transmission of the whooping cough.

Why is this vaccine-preventable disease on the upswing? To answer this question we need to investigate the mechanisms underlying waning protection against pertussis. This requires evaluation of pertussis-specific immune responses over time in wP and aP vaccinated individuals.

CMI-PB Project

Computational Models of Immunity - Pertussis Boost project aims to provide the scientific community with this very information.

They make their data available vis JSON format returning API. We can read this in R with the read_json() function from the jsonlite package:

library(jsonlite)
